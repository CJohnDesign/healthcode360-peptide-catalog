KEY ACTIONS:

ESSENTIAL AMINO ACIDS

Histidine, Isoleucine, Leucine, Lysine, Methionine, Phenylalanine, Threonine, GABA, Tryptophan and Valine. Your

body can not produce these aminos and one must consume them with either food or supplementation,

hence essential. Supplementation is the only way to guarantee that one is getting these essential nutrients.

CONDITIONALLY ESSENTIAL AMINO ACIDS

Arginine, Cysteine, Taurine, Glycine, Glutamine, Proline, and Tyrosine. These are conditional in that your

body does produce them, but may not produce enough at certain times at the needs them, for example,

under pathophysiological conditions such as prematurity in the infant or individuals with severe catabolic

distress.

NON-ESSENTIAL AMINO ACIDS

Alanine, Aspartic Acid, Carnitine, Ornithine, Serine and Glutamic Acid. These amino acids can be

synthesized in sufficient quantities in the body.

BRANCH CHAIN AMINO ACIDS (BCAA)

Valine, Leucine and Isoleucine. These essential nutrients help support muscle metabolism and are

important for building muscle tissue protein.
